Albert Einstein

The man who changed Physics

Timeline for Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1979 - Born in Ulm, German Empire.
  • 1880 – The Einsteins move to Munich.
  • 1896 – Young Albert Einstein enters the physics and mathematics department of the Polytechnic School in the Swiss city of Zurich.
  • 1900 – Einstein receives a diploma as a teacher of physics and mathematics.
  • 1902 – Albert Einstein and Mileva Marić have an illegitimate daughter, Lieserl.
  • 1903 – Albert and Mileva Marić are married.
  • 1904 – The young spouses Albert and Mileva have a son, Hans-Albert.
  • 1905 – A significant year for Einstein: the defense of the dissertation "A New Determination of the Size of Molecules", the publication of other fundamental works: on the photoelectric effect, on the Brownian motion of molecules and the special theory of relativity.
  • 1909 – Einstein becomes an assistant professor of theoretical physics at the University of Zurich.
  • 1910 – Albert and Mileva have a son, Eduard (Tede)
  • 1911 – Albert gets a position as a professor at the Karl-Ferdinand University in Prague.
  • 1912 – Einstein is appointed professor at the Polytechnic in Zurich.
  • 1913 – Albert Einstein is elected a full member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences in Berlin.
  • 1915 – Einstein was the first to formulate the general theory of relativity.
  • 1917 – A special Institute for Physical Research was founded in Berlin under the leadership of A. Einstein.
  • 1919 – A special expedition of the Royal Society of London was organized to study the bending of light rays by solar mass, predicted by Einstein. The beginning of international recognition of the scientist. There is a divorce from Mileva and then a betrothal to his cousin Elsa.
  • 1921 – Einstein organizes the Unemployment Aid Society together with Katja Kolvits, Georg Schau, Clara Zetkin, Anatole France, Maximilian Garden and Heinrich Vogeler. In Potsdam, the grand opening of the observatory designed by Erich Mendelssohn took place (the building later became known as the "Einstein Tower").
  • 1922 – Einstein receives the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1927 – Nobel laureate Albert Einstein takes part in the opening of the University of Jerusalem in Israel.
  • 1929 – Albert and Elsa are building their own house in the town of Kaput, Brandenburg, Germany.
  • 1933 – Albert Einstein was removed from the Prussian Academy of Sciences (even before the decision of the National Socialist authorities), all his property in Germany was confiscated. The scientist and his family moved to the United States, where he became a professor at the Institute of Advanced Technology in Princeton.
  • 1936 - "Elsa, the second wife of the scientist, has died.
  • 1939 – On August 2, Einstein sent a letter to US President Franklin Roosevelt, in which he warned about the possibility of the Nazis developing an atomic bomb.
  • 1941 – An outstanding physicist receives American citizenship.
  • 1945 – In August, Einstein writes another letter to Roosevelt, in which he warns the President of the United States about the catastrophic consequences of the use of atomic weapons. The letter ends up on the president's desk on the day of Franklin Roosevelt's death. The Americans carry out atomic bombings of the Japanese cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Einstein creates an anti-war committee.
  • 1948 – In August, Einstein learns about the death of his first wife Mileva.
  • 1952 – Einstein rejects the offer of Israeli Prime Minister David Ben-Gurion to become president of Israel.
  • 1955 – On April 18, Albert Einstein died in a hospital in Princeton.
